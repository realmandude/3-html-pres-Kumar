TREES FUN FACTS:

1. Use paper wisely. We can save trees from being cut down by using less paper. 2. Play and create with trash. Cardboard boxes, empty toilet paper and paper towel rolls even shoeboxes can be reused. 3. Borrow, share and donate books 4. Plant a tree or other plants. As of 2010, the United States had 304,022,000 hectares (751,255,000 acres) of forested lands, a number that represents one-third of the country. Of this area, 25 percent is old growth forest, 67 percent is secondary forest, and 8 percent is tree farms or plantations.

Trees

Most of the Earth contains a third of forests which is greater than 700 million acres in the United States. Forests are habitats to many different plants and animals. They contribute to people around the world with supplies like food, fuel, medicine, etc. Trees absorb carbon monoxide and other harfmul gasses in the atmosphere and release oxygen. With more trees being cut down every year results in carbon monoxide pollution to increase and the oxygen level to decrease.
